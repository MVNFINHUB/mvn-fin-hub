How markets work

Markets aggregate prices based on supply, demand and expectations. Prices reflect available information and sentiment â€” which can change quickly when new information arrives.

Key idea

Price is a snapshot of what market participants are willing to pay now given information and expectations about the future.

When earnings reports surprise a company, its stock price may move as participants update expectations.

Narratives vs facts

Markets are shaped by stories (narratives) and data (facts). Distinguishing between catchy narratives and robust evidence helps reduce reactionary decisions.

Exercise

Pick a recent market headline and list what is a narrative, what is evidence, and what additional information would help form a view.

Practical examples

Short case studies to practise reading data and understanding market moves.

  1. Read a recent CPI release and summarise the key takeaways in three lines.
  2. Compare two short-term rates and explain possible reasons for the gap.
