Understanding debt

Debt lets you access goods or capital earlier but carries cost (interest) and risk. Distinguish between productive debt (e.g., human capital, home) and high-cost consumer debt.

Practical tip

Prioritise high-interest debt repayment while keeping an emergency buffer.

Exercise

List your outstanding debts, interest rates and monthly commitments; identify which to prioritise by cost and impact.

Insurance basics

Insurance transfers specific risks to an insurer in exchange for a premium. Understand coverage, exclusions, deductibles, and whether the risk is worth transferring.

Concept

Buy insurance for risks that would cause large financial harm relative to the premium cost.

Example

Home insurance protects against large losses (fire, theft) that could otherwise be financially devastating.

Think in time horizons, expected living costs, and the role of compounding. Prioritise consistent saving and simple diversification rather than timing markets.

Start early, contribute regularly, and focus on costs and allocation over short-term returns.

Decision frameworks

Use simple frameworks: define objective, list options, estimate outcomes, account for uncertainty, and choose a robust option. Document reasons and revisit periodically.

Exercise

Pick a personal finance choice (e.g., loan vs savings) and write a 3-step decision note describing your choice and risks.
